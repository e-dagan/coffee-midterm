V60 Pour Over

Origins

The V60 is a popular pour over device that usually uses a paper filter. The pour over can yield a very clean, light, and flavorful cup of coffee often enjoyed either hot or iced.

Brewing Type

It may be observed, especially when using a tall, narrow carafe, that the coffee at the bottom of the coffeepot is stronger than that at the top. This is because less flavor is available for extraction from the coffee grounds as the brewing process progresses. Design Features

The V60 comes from the coffee product company Hario. There are many different designs of this type of coffee maker. Materials range from glass, metal, and even ceramics. Some of the brewers have a zig zag design. These edges can help the filter paper stay in place.

Coffee

Brewing with a paper filter produces clear, light-bodied coffee. While free of sediments, such coffee is lacking in some of coffee's oils and essences; they have been trapped in the paper filter. Metal filters do not remove these components.
